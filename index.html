Three-minute Training

【24】Students, next week you will interview professionals. Today I speak about Mehrabian’s rule. This rule is about feelings and attitude. It says three parts work together: words (about 7%), voice (about 38%), and body language (about 55%). When words and feelings do not match, people trust voice and body more than words. So, your tone and your body are very important. This rule is a guide, not a perfect law for every talk, but it can help you make a strong interview. Use this idea in your interview. Your words should be simple and clear. Your voice should be warm and calm. Your body should show respect. Sit up straight. Smile. Look at their eyes sometimes. Nod to show “I understand.” Do not cross your arms. Keep your hands relaxed. These small actions can spark trust. Listen with care. Use short phrases: “I see,” “Thank you,” “That’s interesting.” Repeat key ideas to check: “So, you started this job in 2019, right?” Ask open questions and let the other person speak. In the end, say, “Thank you for your time. I learned a lot.” Remember: words matter, but voice and body often guide feelings. Let your words be simple, your tone kind, and your body language positive.
